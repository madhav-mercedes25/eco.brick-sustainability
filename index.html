What Are Ecobricks?

Ecobricks are plastic bottles packed tightly with non-biodegradable waste such as wrappers, bags, and packaging. They prevent this waste from entering landfills, waterways, or being incinerated, which helps reduce pollution and protect the environment.

How to Make an Ecobrick

  1. Find a clean, dry plastic bottle.
  2. Collect clean, dry non-recyclable plastic waste (wrappers, bags, etc.).
  3. Use a stick to pack the plastic tightly into the bottle.
  4. Ensure the bottle is solid and heavy when finished.
  5. Label it with your name and weight if needed. Store it safely.

Impact of Ecobricks

From 1950 to 2020, global plastic production surged from 2 million to nearly 500 million tonnes. It is projected to reach 838 million tonnes by 2035. Ecobricks offer a practical solution to reuse this non-recyclable plastic safely and productively.

Our Progress So Far: We've successfully created six Ecobricks and continue to spread awareness on their environmental benefits. Small actions like this can protect millions of terrestrial and aquatic species.
